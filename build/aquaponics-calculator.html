Compatibility is temperature-based: the fish's comfort midpoint sets the shared-loop temperature, and each crop is rated by whether that falls in its preferred (good), tolerable (workable) or outside (poor) root-zone range. "Poor" pairs aren't impossible — they imply a decoupled design with separate water loops. Monthly cash-flow model: year 0 = investment; fish income starts after the grow-out lag (continuous stocking), crops after the first cycle, costs from month 1 (full feed, slightly conservative). Break-even = first month cumulative turns positive. Excluded: financing/interest, tax, working capital, permits/effluent/hygiene. High-value fish/crustaceans (pikeperch, prawn, noble crayfish) are indicative niche species. Defaults are regional point-estimates — climate, energy, wages, rents, per-species prices and subsidy programs all switch with the Region selector and are sourced per data/regions/<region>.yaml (see each region's notes and the subsidy source links for provenance). All values are in the selected region's local currency; the small "≈ €" under the headline numbers is a rough, comparison-only conversion (a dated FX rate in each region file — not a quote). Verify figures before relying on them.
