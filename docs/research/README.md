# Aquaponics feasibility research

Per-species / per-crop real-world feasibility, researched from grower forums (r/aquaponics,
Backyard Aquaponics, aquaponik-forum.de), blogs, user accounts and commercial case studies.
One research agent per species/crop + a synthesis pass; gathered 2026-06 for the
Berlin/Brandenburg context. Each page cross-checks the calculator defaults in `src/data/`.

## Fish ranking (most → least feasible, Brandenburg small commercial)

| # | Species | Verdict | Rationale |
|---|---------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | [African catfish](fish-catfish.md) | ✅ proven | Only 'proven' fish: multiple operating German commercial RAS farms (Sukow ~400 t/yr, Frankenwels ~120 t/yr, Brandenburg's Obermuehle), extremely hardy/high-density, fast ~5-month grow-out, FCR ~0.9, juveniles readily bought from DE/NL suppliers at 15-20 cents. The dominant risk is heat (needs 27-28C; every viable farm uses FREE biogas waste heat) and price reality (~8 EUR/kg at scale vs model's 9). Best husbandry-to-evidence match for a Brandenburg coupled farm, with heating cost the only serious caveat. |
| 2 | [Carp](fish-carp.md) | 🟢 viable | Best-documented European low-tech species and native (no invasive-species friction). Brandenburg is the literal carp heartland, so K1 juveniles are cheap and local, and 'Weihnachtskarpfen' direct-sale demand is genuinely strong. A documented German practitioner already raises Spiegelkarpfen in aquaponics incl. outdoor overwintering. Hardy and forgiving. Downsides: slow growth (true market carp is ~3 summers, model's 18mo yields only 0.8-1.2kg), winter feeding shutdown below 10C, geosmin off-flavour needing purging, and highly seasonal demand. |
| 3 | [Trout](fish-trout.md) | 🟢 viable | Canonical cold-climate aquaponics fish, ideal for Brandenburg winters with minimal heating; fingerlings readily bought from many DE/AT hatcheries. Solid familiar high-value market (Forelle), with smoked/fillet value-add premiums. Ranked below carp/catfish because of the inverse climate risk: continental summers push water above 18-22C, requiring active cooling or cool groundwater, plus high DO demand is a single-point-of-failure. The Berlin Baganz study warns carnivore coupled aquaponics is hard to profit below ~2000 m2, and 14 EUR/kg only holds via direct/value-added sale. |
| 4 | [Tilapia](fish-tilapia.md) | 🟢 viable | Most-documented aquaponics fish globally, hardy and fast while warm, with real German demand (Kirschauer, Obermuehle). But the winter-heating economics are the documented make-or-break: tilapia stall below 20C and die ~10-13C, so Brandenburg means heating Oct-Apr, which is precisely why commercial European tilapia has largely failed and a German project (EigenEnergie) abandoned it. Juvenile sourcing is a real constraint (essentially only Til Aqua NL or self-breed) and it is an alien species needing biosecurity permits. Viable but economically fragile. |
| 5 | [Pikeperch (Zander)](fish-pikeperch.md) | 🟠 challenging | Germany is the global center of farmed zander; excellent demand and pricing (~27 EUR/kg whole, ~36 fillet), proven in research aquaponics rigs, juveniles buyable (vde-zander, Kaiserzander, AquaPri DK), and EU grant co-funding is realistic. Buying juveniles sidesteps the brutal ~10% fry-survival bottleneck. Ranked mid because every documented success is a professionally engineered RAS not a typical coupled setup; it is stress-sensitive, water-quality-exacting, needs year-round 23-28C heating, and trade press says a RAS-zander market breakthrough is 'not yet in sight.' |
| 6 | [European perch](fish-perch.md) | 🟠 challenging | Native premium restaurant fish ('Egli') with strong DACH demand and a Berlin research hub (IGB, PERCIPONIE); ECF Berlin actually runs perch commercially. But real coupled-aquaponics evidence is thin and cautionary: only works if you BUY weaned pellet-trained domesticated juveniles (scarce, ~10% hatchery survival, expensive), wild strains grow too slowly and refuse pellets, and it needs constant warm 22-25C heating through Brandenburg winters. High price (16 EUR/kg) hinges entirely on the direct-restaurant channel vs ~5 EUR/kg wholesale. Difficulty likely understated at 'Medium.' |
| 7 | [Arctic char](fish-archar.md) | 🟠 challenging | Biologically the lowest-heating premium salmonid and an excellent Brandenburg-winter fit; juveniles (Saibling Setzlinge) cheaply sourced and late-maturing strains exist. Recognized premium gastronomy name supporting ~17 EUR/kg direct. But sobering real-world record: the flagship Urban Organics/Pentair aquaponics deployment was shut in 2019 with the fish program never coming online. Recurring killer is early sexual maturation ruining flesh, plus shy picky feeding and summer water >19C risk. Char in coupled aquaponics is rarely attempted domestically; 'Medium' difficulty is optimistic. |
| 8 | [Noble crayfish (Edelkrebs)](fish-noblecray.md) | 🟠 challenging | High-value native niche with real German R&D backing and easy juvenile sourcing (~1.80-3.80 EUR/pc), and price upside (~60 EUR/kg vs model's 35). But almost no evidence is true coupled aquaponics, growth is brutally slow (4-5 years/48-60mo to market, not the model's 30mo), it halts below 12C so Brandenburg loses ~half the year, cannibalism caps density at 1-2/m2, and insiders describe the sector as shrinking ('am Absterben'). A research/premium-niche play, not a scalable coupled-aquaponics business. |
| 9 | [Sturgeon](fish-sturgeon.md) | 🟠 challenging | Biology proven and strong Brandenburg ecosystem (Zippelsforde, Desietra) with cool-water climate fit and buyable fingerlings. But it is a deep-pocket, long-horizon CAVIAR play that doesn't match a 24-month meat model: meat is a low-value byproduct (~14 EUR/kg) while real money is roe at year 5-12. Extreme oxygen sensitivity (documented crash killing 8/60 fish in 2 hours), large tank/infrastructure needs, capital lock-up, and CITES compliance. Challenging with a value proposition mismatched to the calculator's window. |
| 10 | [Redclaw crayfish](fish-redclaw.md) | 🟠 challenging | Hobbyists keep it alive easily but the universal industry verdict is that tank/aquaponics culture is a poor money-maker: viable redclaw is earthen-pond based (depends on pond-bottom detritus), with heavy juvenile cannibalism and low tank area-yield. Non-native tropical species needing year-round 24-30C heating (dies below ~13C), so Brandenburg = continuous energy cost. German activity is tiny indoor-RAS niche only; 25 EUR/kg is well above the ~11-12 EUR/kg commodity price and demand for redclaw specifically is unproven (German premium market is native Astacus at ~60 EUR/kg). |
| 11 | [Giant river prawn](fish-prawn.md) | 🟠 challenging | Lowest feasibility. Warmth-hungry (26-30C year-round heating), strongly cannibalistic during molt, escapes, eats plant roots and gets predated by fish so it must run in a separate low-density decoupled tank, undercutting the coupled premise. On-farm breeding is impossible (larvae need brackish water), so you are a perpetual juvenile buyer at hobby pricing (~12 EUR/animal from Rheingarnelen), implying juvenile cost an order of magnitude above the model's 3.5 EUR/kg. Heterogeneous growth leaves ~50% undersized. Premium price (28 EUR/kg) is real but volumes are tiny. |

## Crop ranking

| # | Species | Verdict | Rationale |
|---|---------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | [Mixed leafy greens](crop-greens_mix.md) | ✅ proven | The single most-documented, most-recommended, highest-margin (~46%) aquaponics category and the safest commercial bet. Light feeders thriving on dilute coupled nutrient loads, standard beginner DWC/raft, 4-6 week cycles, trivial seed sourcing, and strong German demand for regional pesticide-free salad supporting ~4.5 EUR/kg. Caveats are known and manageable (iron dosing, bolting, fish-temperature match favoring cool-water species). 28 kg/m2/yr is best-case requiring winter lighting, but the category's proven status makes it rank 1. |
| 2 | [Baby leaf lettuce](crop-lettuce_leaf.md) | ✅ proven | The benchmark easy aquaponics crop, with the most direct Berlin proof: IGB research matched hydroponic yields (~325g/head) and ECF Farmsystems sells aquaponic salad into ~140 REWE stores across Berlin/Brandenburg. Fast, high cycle count, good margins, trivial seed sourcing. Defaults are slightly optimistic (real cycles 28-49 days not 25; real ideal root zone 20-24C; winter low-DLI needs LED), and the cool/warm fish-temperature compromise is the main agronomic tension. |
| 3 | [Basil](crop-basil.md) | ✅ proven | Most-documented and most-profitable aquaponics herb, with the most Berlin-relevant evidence: a peer-reviewed Northern Germany study grew basil on African catfish effluent over 41-day cycles, and ECF Berlin standardized on basil commercially. Fast cycles, repeated cuts, strong demand at ~14 EUR/kg. Ranked just below the salads because of serious disease risk: Pythium root rot and explosively-spreading basil downy mildew have caused total crop loss. 18 kg/m2/yr needs strong winter light; pairs naturally with warm-water catfish/tilapia. |
| 4 | [Head lettuce](crop-lettuce_head.md) | 🟢 viable | Lettuce is the most successful aquaponics crop and Brandenburg's cool climate fits head lettuce's 15-21C band most of the year, avoiding the heat-bolting that cripples warm-climate growers. Seed/slow-bolt cultivars trivial to source. Ranked below leaf lettuce because dense head-forming types are a notch harder (tipburn, head-closure failure, summer-greenhouse bolting risk) and labour is the dominant cost the model under-weights (~65-69% of variable cost). Note ECF chose basil+perch over lettuce, implying thin commodity-lettuce margins. |
| 5 | [Cutting herbs](crop-herbs.md) | 🟢 viable | Cutting herbs are consistently among the most profitable aquaponics crops with proven Berlin retail channels (ECF 'Hauptstadtbasilikum' into ~450 supermarkets) and ~12 EUR/kg direct-to-chef pricing. Mint and parsley are reliable vigorous performers. Ranked mid because the named trio is uneven: coriander is the outlier (bolts fast, ~50-55 day turn, wants cooler water than a fish tank gives), and mint is aggressively invasive needing physical isolation labour. Recommend down-weighting coriander; the flagship Berlin farm tellingly chose basil instead. |
| 6 | [Swiss chard](crop-chard.md) | 🟢 viable | Robust, cold/frost-tolerant, bolt-resistant 'starter' green with strong real data: a peer-reviewed low-tech CARP aquaponics study hit 5.33 kg/m2 in ~70 days, beating the hydroponic control by ~38%. Excellent winter/shoulder-season fit for cool fish-set water, trivial seed sourcing (Mangold is a German staple). Ranked here because single-cycle yield is only ~5 kg/m2, so the 30 kg/m2/yr default is optimistic (15-25 safer), and it is a lower-volume specialty green suited to direct/restaurant rather than wholesale. |
| 7 | [Rocket / Rucola](crop-rocket.md) | 🟢 viable | Consistently recommended fast leafy green (first cut ~21-28 days), low nutrient demand, shallow roots ideal for raft/NFT, established German salad-mix demand. Cold-tolerant, good for cool-water-fish Brandenburg systems. Ranked mid-pack because temperature-driven bolting/bitterness above ~21-24C makes it strictly dependent on cool-water fish (tilapia/catfish would ruin it), it is a flea-beetle magnet as a brassica, and 11 EUR/kg only holds for premium organic packaged direct sale (vs ~2.20-6.60/kg conventional). |
| 8 | [Spinach](crop-spinach.md) | 🟢 viable | A genuine but conditional crop: succeeds only in COOL water (10-16C) with cold-water fish like trout, and bolts/rots (Pythium aphanidermatum) in warm tilapia systems, so the fish choice gates feasibility entirely. Notorious germination failure (thermal dormancy) and tight margins add friction. Winter is its strength (cool, bolt-suppressing). 24 kg/m2/yr is optimistic (needs intensive dense baby-leaf cycling); demand for Babyspinat is real but a grower warned not all markets pay a favorable price. |
| 9 | [Pak choi](crop-pakchoi.md) | 🟢 viable | Fast, beginner-friendly Asian green with real Berlin restaurant demand (~6 EUR/kg) and easy seed sourcing. Ranked low because of two well-documented Brandenburg-specific traps a German forum grower hit: it bolts readily under both cold stress AND long-day winter supplementary light (so naive winter lighting backfires; cap photoperiod ~10-12h), and aphids can wipe out the brassica crop in days with no pesticide option in a coupled loop. 28 kg/m2/yr is optimistic ceiling; realistic ~10-20 after bolting/pest losses. |
| 10 | [Lamb's lettuce (Feldsalat)](crop-feldsalat.md) | 🟢 viable | Genuinely cold-hardy and one of few crops producing through cold-water-fish winters, with strong real premium pricing (Bioland direct-sale 24-35 EUR/kg, far above model's 13) validating a winter-premium thesis. But ranked near the bottom because it is a niche, not a workhorse: tiny slow-growing plant, low absolute yield (12 kg/m2/yr too high), very narrow harvest window, and multiple growers judge commercial production 'kaum lohnenswert' once lighting/labour are counted. Works as a premium winter complement, not a core crop. |
| 11 | [Microgreens](crop-microgreens.md) | 🟠 challenging | Lowest feasibility for COUPLED aquaponics specifically: microgreens grow off their own seed reserves in 7-14 days, barely touch fish-water nutrients and 'support very few fish,' so the core fish-waste-fertilises-plants value proposition fails. Serious operators (e.g. Goldenleafs Nurnberg) grow them in soil/decoupled. Worse, putting raw recirculating fish water on a raw ready-to-eat crop is a recognized EU food-safety risk heavily regulated after the deadly 2011 German STEC sprout outbreak. Price (40 EUR/kg) is real but it behaves like decoupled hydro on a flood table, not aquaponics. |

## Cross-cutting themes

- WINTER HEATING IS THE DOMINANT, OFTEN-UNMODELLED COST. For every warm-water species (catfish, tilapia, pikeperch, perch, redclaw, prawn) the make-or-break economic variable in Brandenburg is heating water Oct-Apr, not fish husbandry. The only profitable German/Austrian warm-water farms found (Frankenwels, Sukow) survive by co-locating with biogas plants for FREE waste heat. A standalone build must budget substantial winter heating energy the price/FCR defaults do not capture.
- FISH TEMPERATURE GATES CROP CHOICE IN A COUPLED LOOP. Because fish set the water temperature, crop feasibility is downstream of the fish decision. Cool-water fish (trout, carp, perch, char) match the cool root zones that lettuce, rocket, spinach, chard and Feldsalat need; warm-water fish (tilapia, catfish) suit basil and warm herbs but cause bolting/Pythium in salads. Spinach and rocket in particular are only feasible with cool-water fish. The calculator should treat fish-crop temperature pairing as a hard compatibility constraint, not two independent choices.
- GROW-OUT TIMES ARE OFTEN MIS-ESTIMATED IN BOTH DIRECTIONS, AND WINTER STOPS THE CLOCK. Fast warm-water fish (catfish ~5mo vs modelled 7; pikeperch ~14-18mo vs 20) are modelled too slow, while slow native species (carp true market 3yr, noble crayfish 4-5yr vs modelled 30mo) are modelled far too fast. Critically, unheated species essentially stop growing below their feeding threshold (carp <10C, noble crayfish <12C), so in Brandenburg the effective grow-out clock only runs in the warm season unless the loop is heated year-round.
- PREMIUM DIRECT/RESTAURANT PRICING IS THE ONLY WAY THE HEADLINE PRICES HOLD; WHOLESALE IS FAR LOWER. Nearly every high price (catfish 9, trout 14, perch 16, pikeperch 22, char 17, rocket 11, Feldsalat, microgreens) is realistic only via direct-to-consumer, farm-gate, restaurant or value-added (smoked/fillet) channels. Wholesale/commodity prices are routinely 30-70% lower. Profitability therefore depends as much on securing a direct-sales channel and capturing eco/regional premiums as on production yield.
- JUVENILE/SEED SOURCING IS EASY FOR NATIVE AND ESTABLISHED SPECIES BUT A REAL BOTTLENECK FOR EXOTICS. Catfish, carp, trout, char, perch, pikeperch and noble crayfish have solid DE/EU juvenile supply matching the 'bought not bred' assumption. Tilapia (essentially only Til Aqua NL + MOQ), prawn (hobby-priced ~12 EUR/animal, no on-farm breeding possible) and weaned percid juveniles are scarce/expensive, materially inflating real juvenile cost above modelled defaults.
- COUPLED AQUAPONICS PROFITABILITY IS UNPROVEN BELOW A SCALE THRESHOLD, AND BERLIN COMMERCIAL OPERATORS VOTE WITH THEIR PRODUCT MIX. The Berlin/IGB Baganz multi-loop study shows coupled aquaponics is hard to profit below ~2000 m2, and the flagship commercial operator ECF Farmsystems standardized on BASIL + PERCH/lettuce rather than commodity crops or exotic fish, a strong real-world signal about what actually pays in this exact market.
- EU ORGANIC (BIO) CERTIFICATION IS GENERALLY UNAVAILABLE FOR SOILLESS AQUAPONIC PRODUCE, CAPPING THE PRICE PREMIUM. EU organic rules require soil-based cultivation, so aquaponic greens/herbs typically cannot be sold as certified Bio and must be positioned as 'regional/pesticide-free' instead. This caps the achievable premium versus the Bioland/Naturland prices that drive crops like Feldsalat, and should temper price assumptions across all crops.
- SEVERAL CROPS BREAK THE COUPLED-AQUAPONICS PREMISE OR CARRY HIDDEN REGULATORY/LABOUR BURDENS. Microgreens barely consume nutrients (functionally decoupled hydro) and raise an acute EU food-safety issue putting raw fish water on raw ready-to-eat produce post the 2011 STEC outbreak; mint is invasive and needs isolation labour; winter supplementary lighting can paradoxically TRIGGER bolting in pak choi and rocket via long-day photoperiod, so 'just add winter light' is wrong without capping the photoperiod.
