# Noble crayfish (Edelkrebs) — aquaponics feasibility

> **Verdict: 🟠 challenging** · kind: fish · data id: `noblecray`
>
> Community-evidence research (forums, grower blogs, user accounts, commercial cases), 2026-06.

## Summary

Noble crayfish (Astacus astacus) is a genuinely high-value native niche, and there are real German practitioner and research efforts (the BMBF-funded Wagemann felchen+Edelkrebs polyculture, AWI/Bremerhaven RAS trials, several commercial Edelkrebs farms), so the species is not exotic in Germany. But almost none of the real-world evidence is aquaponics in the coupled-loop sense growers use the word: it is open-pond extensive culture or research RAS, and the dominant reported reality is brutally slow growth (4-5 years to ~80-120 g market size, not 30 months) plus cannibalism around molting that caps stocking density. Practitioners and researchers agree noble crayfish only grows when water is above ~12°C and best at 18-22°C, which in unheated Berlin/Brandenburg means roughly half the year of zero growth, pushing realistic grow-out toward the high end. The economics are saved only by very high price (commercial farms quote ~€60/kg retail to Sterneküche, above the model's €35), but the sector is described by insiders as shrinking ("am Absterben"), so demand is thin and bespoke rather than scalable.

## Pros

- Genuinely high value: German/Austrian farms quote ~€60/kg retail Speisekrebs (model's €35/kg may be conservative for direct sale to fine dining)
- Native and cold-tolerant: survives 5-25°C, no tropical heating needed to keep alive (unlike tilapia), and a German aquaponics operator (Bioponic Schiller) reportedly switched from tilapia to Astacus for exactly this reason
- Excellent survival in controlled RAS: AWI trials reported 100% survival in RAS vs ~70% in ponds, and disease-free closed systems avoid the crayfish-plague risk that devastates native stock
- Behaviorally complementary in polyculture: the BMBF Wagemann project paired bottom-dwelling Edelkrebs with mid-water felchen, the crayfish eating uneaten feed and partly processing fish faeces (a real aquaponics-relevant function)
- Juveniles are readily bought in Germany/Austria (Sömmerlinge ~€1.80/pc, 2-summer ~€3.80/pc), so the bought-not-bred assumption is realistic
- Strong premium-niche demand exists at the top (Michelin/Sterneküche buyers, restocking market)

## Cons

- Extremely slow growth: commercial farms and fisheries sources report 4-5 years to reach 80-120 g / 12 cm market size, far beyond the model's 30 months
- Growth stops below 12°C water (no molting) and optimum is 18-22°C; unheated Brandenburg winters mean ~6 months/year of no growth, so 30-month grow-out is only conceivable with year-round heating, which erodes the cost advantage
- Cannibalism of freshly-molted animals is the #1 mortality cause in crowded stocks; pond densities are only 1-2 animals/m², so high-density aquaponics tanks need extensive shelter and still risk losses
- Almost no first-hand COUPLED aquaponics success stories found; evidence is research RAS or extensive ponds, and the one cited permies query about European crayfish got 'no experience' as the only reply
- RAS growth was significantly slower than ponds (SGR 0.78 vs 1.23) and yielded lower-lipid, lower-quality meat in trials
- Low edible yield (only tail and claws eaten) limits effective €/kg of sellable meat
- Native species is protection-regulated; restocked/wild populations cannot be harvested, and farming is constrained vs invasive species
- German breeder insiders describe the whole sector as shrinking (~60 breeders nationwide, 'am Absterben'), signaling a thin, bespoke market rather than a scalable one

## Community evidence

- **[academic]** Bioökonomie.de / BMBF Wagemann polyculture (Edelkrebs + Felchen) https://biooekonomie.de/foerderung/foerderbeispiele/aquakultur-fisch-und-edelkrebs-einer-wg
  — BMBF-funded (€50k sondage + €1.1M, 30-month feasibility) coupled polyculture: felchen are heavy feed-wasters and Edelkrebs eats the leftovers and partly processes feces; no infectious disease appeared. But size-matching is critical (large crayfish predate small fish) and the worked example was a single Black Forest 3-star restaurant for internal supply/restocking, not scaled production.
- **[commercial-case]** Krebsgarten / lebensmittelmagazin.de https://www.lebensmittelmagazin.de/wirtschaft/20200724-im-krebsgang-edelkrebse-aus-mecklenburg-vorpommern/
  — Commercial farm (Crivitz, MV, 3.5 ha, ~20,000 animals): crayfish need 3-5 years to reach 80-120 g; ~€60/kg, viable only for upscale Sterneküche since only tail/claws are eaten; recent mink predation losses; sector described as 'am Absterben' with only ~60 breeders nationwide.
- **[academic]** AWI/Bremerhaven RAS growth study, Astacus astacus (EPIC/Springer Aquaculture International) https://epic.awi.de/id/eprint/37708/
  — RAS vs open pond over 2 months: RAS survival 100% vs ~70% in ponds, but RAS growth slower (SGR 0.78 at 5% bw/day vs pond 1.23) and lower lipid content. Authors argue annualized RAS performance and disease-free, high-value output may offset slower short-term growth with optimized diets.
- **[academic]** Reducing/enhancing survival in crowded Astacus astacus indoor RAS (ResearchGate) https://www.researchgate.net/publication/261530159
  — Cannibalism of freshly-molted (asynchronously molting) individuals is the main mortality driver in crowded indoor stocks; mitigated by abundant daytime shelters and continuous/adjusted lighting to reduce agonistic behavior. Cannibalism intensifies at sexual maturity (year 2-3).
- **[commercial-case]** Waldviertler Besatz- und Speisekrebszucht price list https://www.edelkrebszucht.at/preise/
  — Concrete sourcing/pricing: Sömmerlinge (2-3 cm) €1.80/pc, 2-summer (4-7 cm) €3.80/pc, mature €4.80/pc, egg-bearing females €18/pc; Speisekrebs ab 80 g €60/kg (often sold out). Min order 50 pcs; ships to Germany (~€60 shipping, €200 min) — confirms juveniles are buyable, not bred.
- **[blog]** Flusskrebszucht Stephan Frömel / edelkrebse-shop Steckbrief + Bavarian fisheries (LfU/LfV Bayern) https://www.fluss-krebse.de/der-edelkrebs/ ; https://lfvbayern.de/lexikon/edelkrebs
  — Husbandry facts from breeders/fisheries: optimum 18-22°C (briefly to ~26°C max), NO molting below 12°C, needs 2-3 months at 16°C+ for gonad development; ~4 years hatch-to-table, market animals 125 g (female) to 250 g (male); legal minimum size 12 cm. Confirms slow growth and cold-season standstill.
- **[forum]** Permies aquaponics forum — European freshwater crayfish thread https://permies.com/t/48604/experience-European-freshwater-crayfish
  — A grower (Belgium) explicitly asked for first-hand experience with European freshwater crayfish in aquaponics; the only reply (from author Sylvia Bernstein) was 'No, unfortunately I don't.' Illustrates how little coupled-aquaponics practitioner experience exists for this species.
- **[blog]** Crayfish in aquaponics general reports (ouraquaponics blog, growertoday, leaffin) http://ouraquaponics.blogspot.com/2013/10/raising-crayfish-in-aquaponics.html
  — Generic crayfish-in-aquaponics accounts (mostly non-noble species) consistently flag escape/containment problems (animals climbing out into adjacent water overnight) and the need for hiding structures — practical issues that apply doubly to a slow, cannibalistic species like Astacus astacus.

## Model check (vs `src/data` defaults)

Significant discrepancies. (1) Grow-out time: model says 30 months; commercial farms and fisheries sources consistently report 4-5 years (48-60 months) hatch-to-table to reach 80-120 g / 12 cm — and that is in pond conditions. In unheated Brandenburg, growth halts below 12°C water, so ~6 months/year contribute nothing; realistic grow-out from bought juveniles to market is multi-year, not 2.5 years, unless the system is heated year-round (which the cost model presumably doesn't assume). (2) Temperature band: model's 15-20°C is slightly low — growers cite 18-22°C optimum with no molting below 12°C, so 15°C is sub-optimal for growth. (3) Price: model's €35/kg direct-sale is conservative; Austrian/German farms quote ~€60/kg retail Speisekrebs, though edible yield is low (tail+claws only) and the buyer pool is narrow (fine dining). (4) FCR 1.8: no first-hand FCR figure surfaced; RAS feeding was run at up to 5% bw/day with slow growth, hinting effective FCR in cold/slow conditions could be worse than 1.8, but this is unconfirmed. (5) Juvenile cost: €4/kg-produced is plausible — Sömmerlinge at €1.80/pc and 2-summer at €3.80/pc are documented German/Austrian prices. Difficulty 'Hard' is well-supported (cannibalism, low density 1-2/m², slow growth, cold-season standstill).

## Germany / EU notes

Highly Germany/EU-relevant species. Juvenile sourcing is solid: multiple German/Austrian breeders sell Satzkrebse/Sömmerlinge (~€1.80-3.80/pc) and ship within DE (e.g. Waldviertler AT, Frömel MV, edelkrebse-shop, Krebsgarten Basthorst). Climate fit is the core problem for Berlin/Brandenburg: Astacus astacus tolerates 5-25°C and survives cold, but does NOT grow or molt below 12°C and needs sustained 16°C+ summers for reproduction — so unheated outdoor/greenhouse systems lose ~half the year of growth, stretching an already 4-5-year grow-out. Regulation: native, protection-regulated; restocked/wild animals can't be harvested, and the invasive-species crayfish-plague threat is a real biosecurity reason to favor closed RAS. Market demand is genuine but niche and apparently contracting — insiders cite ~60 breeders nationwide and call the sector 'am Absterben'; buyers are predominantly upscale/Sterneküche plus a restocking market, not volume retail. Real German R&D backing exists (BMBF Bioökonomie polyculture, AWI Bremerhaven RAS studies, INRM), confirming feasibility interest but also that this remains a research/premium-niche play rather than a proven coupled-aquaponics business.
